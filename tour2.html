The Significance of 5 Manache Ganpati and Dagdusheth Ganpati in Pune

|| ॐ श्री गणेशाय नमः ||

Every individual knows the history behind the start of Ganesh Festival. Ganesh Utsav was confined to the individual homes and palaces since Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj period until Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak, started the concept of “Sarvajanik Ganeshotsav”.

Today every city in Maharashtra has their own way of celebrating the most awaited Ganesh festival. However, Pune, the pioneer of Sarvajanik Ganeshotsav carries a different significance. People have heard about the most worshipped 5 Manache Ganpati in Pune along with Dagdusheth Ganpati. However, many of us are not fully aware of their significance.

Let’s go through the significance of each of the 5 Manache Ganpati in Pune and Dagdusheth Ganpati.

1. Kasba Ganpati

Kasba Ganpati from Kasba Peth, Pune is the 1st amongst the Manache Ganpati. It is knows as a presiding deity of Pune as the temple was built by Shivaji Maharaj & Jijabai Bhosale in the year 1639. Earlier Kasba Ganpati celebrated the festival within the premises until 1925. From 1926 till date, it is celebrated in an enclosed mandap. The mandal also organizes a ‘Mela’ that encourages the singers, artists and performers.

2. Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati

Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati is the 2nd in the list of Manache Ganpati in Pune. Tambdi Jogeshwari is the goddess, Peshwas used to seek the blessing from this one of the oldest temples in Pune. Every year, the mandal makes the same replica of the idol. The face of the idol resembles the African elephant. The sculptor of the ideal, DM Gulunjkar, sculpted the idol at the beginning. Now, the 4th generation of Gulunjkar family is continuing this legacy.  

3. Guruji Talim Ganpati

Guruji Talim Ganpati, the oldest Ganpati Mandal in Pune, was started in 1887. Talim refers to the wrestling training. The mandal was backed by the Guruji Talim. The mandal played a key role in spreading the message of communal harmony on the background of the Hindu-Muslim riot in 1893. The mandal was given the third place in the immersion procession in 1950. You can experience the celebration at Ganpati Chowk.

4. Tulshibaug Ganpati

The 4th Ganpati is Tulshibaug Ganpati. Started in 1901, this was the first Mandal to start the decoration in front of the idols. This mandal is the pioneer of the large size idol in Pune. Tulshibaug was famous for the ancient Lord Ram temple. Due to the religious importance, this Ganpati is considered as the 4th Manacha Ganpati.

5. Kesari Wada Ganpati

Kesari Wada Ganpati was founded by Lokmanya Tilak. Kesari Wada Ganesh Mandal is considered as one of the most respected Ganesh Mandals in Pune. The mandal gives emphasises on organising the cultural activities and educational programs instead of spending on decoration. You can visit Gaikwad Wada in Pune to witness the joy at this Ganesh Mandal. The mandal also celebrates the Ganesh festival as per Tilak Panchang.

Dagdusheth Ganpati Mandal

There’s no article on Ganesh Festival in Pune that will be complete without mentioning Dagdusheth Ganpati. Though it is not considered in the list of Manache Ganpati, it has its own significance. Dagdusheth Ganpati was started by Dagdusheth Halwai and his wife Lakshmibai when they lost their only son to the plague epidemic. Earlier, this Ganpati was known as ‘Bahulicha Houd Ganpati’ or ‘Kotwal Chawadi Ganpati’. People from different countries come to Pune to seek the blessing of Dagdusheth Ganpati.

During the visarjan, these mandals have the honour of being the foremost among other Ganpati Mandals in Pune. For the past 126 years, the order has never changed. No other city in India has such a system which is still being carried out meticulously.
